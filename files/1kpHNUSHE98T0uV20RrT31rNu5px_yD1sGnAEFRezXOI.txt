


This SOP outlines the processes, roles, and responsibilities involved in selecting, implementing, managing, and maintaining instructional software within the district. Key areas include: 
● Software Evaluation and Selection 
● Acquisition and Licensing 
● Software Deployment and Integration 
● Security, Privacy, and Compliance 
● Training and Support 
● Monitoring and Review 
Director of AI and BL: 
● Oversees instructional software selection and implementation. 
● Coordinates licensing agreements and budgeting. 
● Ensures alignment with district instructional goals. 
Technology Team: 
● Manages software deployment, system integration, and technical support. ● Ensures compliance with security and privacy requirements. 
Instructional Coaches: 
● Provide input on instructional needs and software effectiveness. 
● Support educators in software integration with classroom practices. 
Teachers and Support Staff: 
● Implement instructional software effectively in classrooms. 
● Participate in training sessions and provide feedback.
1. Software Evaluation and Selection 
● Identify educational needs through collaboration with educators and instructional leaders. 
● Evaluate software options based on educational effectiveness, user-friendliness, and alignment with district standards. 
2. Acquisition and Licensing 
● Review vendor terms and conditions to ensure compliance with district policies and applicable laws (FERPA, COPPA). 
● Secure necessary approvals and purchase licensing in alignment with district budgeting processes. 
3. Software Deployment and Integration 
● Conduct pilot programs as necessary to assess software suitability. 
● Collaborate with technology for seamless integration into existing platforms (LMS, SIS). ● Schedule deployments strategically to minimize classroom disruption. ● Configure single sign-on (SSO) and user permissions. 
4. Security, Privacy, and Compliance 
● Maintain compliance with data privacy standards and district policies. ● Implement regular audits and updates to ensure ongoing security. 
● Develop clear incident response procedures. 
5. Training and Support 
● Schedule initial training and ongoing professional learning. 
● Provide easy access to user guides, FAQs, and troubleshooting resources. ● Maintain a responsive help desk for quick resolution of issues. 
6. Monitoring and Review 
● Regularly collect feedback from teachers and students to assess software effectiveness. ● Use data to make informed decisions on software renewals, upgrades, or discontinuations. 
● Document and share best practices and lessons learned across the district.
Software purchased for districtwide use or as part of curriculum implementation is covered by district budgets. Software purchased at the building level is covered by the building budget, but it is still subject to this procedure.
