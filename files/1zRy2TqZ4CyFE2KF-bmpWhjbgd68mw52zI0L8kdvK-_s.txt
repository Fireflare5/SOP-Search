


To establish a clear, research-based, and collaborative process for adopting curriculum programs at the elementary level in alignment with district goals, Missouri Learning standards, Lindbergh Priority standards and best practices. 
● Executive Directors of Professional Learning/Elementary Education & Assessment/Student Services: Oversee the process, ensure alignment with district priorities, and facilitate decision-making. 
● Curriculum Coordinators: Research potential programs, coordinate pilot implementations, and gather data. Provide professional development and support for piloted programs. 
● Instructional Coaches: Provide professional development and support for piloted programs. 
● Elementary Principals: Support implementation in schools and provide feedback. ● Teachers: Participate in program evaluation, pilot phases, and provide feedback. ● Parents & Community Representatives: Offer insights on curriculum impact and accessibility. 
● Needs Assessment 
a. Analyze student achievement data and teacher feedback. 
b. Identify gaps in the current curriculum. 
c. Align with state standards and research-based best practices. 
● Research & Exploration 
a. Review evidence-based curriculum options. 
b. Consult other districts and experts on best practices.


c. Develop a list of potential programs for consideration. 
● Program Evaluation 
a. Establish an evaluation rubric based on: 
i. Alignment with state standards 
ii. Research efficacy 
iii. Differentiation & accessibility 
iv. Teacher usability 
v. Assessment capabilities 
b. Conduct vendor presentations and material reviews. 
● Pilot Implementation 
a. Select 2-3 programs for a pilot phase. 
b. Provide professional development for pilot teachers. 
c. Collect ongoing data on student progress, teacher feedback, and usability. ● Data Collection & Analysis 
a. Use formative and summative assessments to measure student growth. b. Gather qualitative feedback from teachers, students, and parents. 
c. Compare pilot results and determine effectiveness. 
● Final Selection & Approval 
a. Review data with the curriculum committee. 
b. Present recommendations to district leadership. 
c. Seek Board of Education approval if required. 
● Implementation Plan 
a. Develop a phased rollout strategy. 
b. Provide district-wide professional development. 
c. Monitor implementation and provide ongoing support. 
● Continuous Review & Improvement 
a. Establish regular checkpoints to assess effectiveness. 
b. Gather stakeholder feedback for adjustments. 
c. Plan for periodic curriculum updates in alignment with new research and standards. 
Review Cycle: This procedure will be reviewed every six years or as needed to ensure alignment with best practices and district needs.
