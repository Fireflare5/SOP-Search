


Purpose 
The purpose of this Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) is to outline the processes for monitoring and responding to flagged content identified by the Securly digital safety filtering system for emails and Google documents. This system ensures the safety and well-being of students by identifying potential risks, including self-harm, bullying, violence, or inappropriate content. These SOPs establish clear roles, responsibilities, and response protocols for school administrators, counselors, and district student services personnel to address flagged content effectively and promptly. 
Responsibilities 
System Administrator Responsibilities: 
○ Manage and maintain the Securly filtering system, including user access, configuration, and reporting functionalities. 
○ Monitor flagged content alerts generated by the system. 
○ Escalate alerts to appropriate personnel (counselors, administrators, or district student services) based on content severity. 
○ Ensure the system remains operational, updated, and compliant with district policies and legal standards. 
School Counselor/Social Work Responsibilities: 
○ Respond to flagged content related to self-harm, mental health, bullying, or interpersonal conflicts. 
○ Conduct a student safety check and engage in supportive discussions to assess the level of concern. 
○ Provide appropriate interventions, including counseling sessions, referrals to external mental health services, and communication with parents/guardians as needed. 
○ School staff (admin, counselors, social workers) who follow up on Securly alerts document in Infinite Campus. Document actions taken and monitor/follow up with the student for ongoing concerns. 
Building Administrator Responsibilities:


○ Review escalated flagged content that indicates serious or imminent threats, such as violence, explicit threats, or significant self-harm concerns. 
○ Collaborate with district-level administrators and school counselors to determine appropriate interventions. 
○ Coordinate with law enforcement, mental health agencies, or other external partners when necessary. 
○ Oversee compliance with legal reporting requirements and district safety policies.This may include suicide risk and behavior risk assessments, and/or mandatory reporting for neglect/abuse if applicable
