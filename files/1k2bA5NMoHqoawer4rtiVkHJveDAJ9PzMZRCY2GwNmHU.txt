


This SOP defines roles, responsibilities, and procedures for administrators managing the Canvas Learning Management System (LMS) at our institution. It includes: 
● Account and User Management 
● Course and Content Management 
● Technical Support and Troubleshooting 
● Data and Analytics 
● Integration and Customization 
● Training and Support

1. Account and User Management 
1.1 Configure and Customize the Canvas Instance 
● Access the Administrator Settings to adjust branding, themes, and system settings to align with the institution’s identity and requirements. 
● Create and manage sub-accounts to reflect our hierarchy. 
1.2 Manage User Roles and Permissions 
● Create or edit roles to match the district needs. 
● Assign permissions carefully to maintain proper access control while allowing necessary functionality. 
1.3 Oversee User Accounts 
● Onboarding New Users: 
○ Coordinate with HR to retrieve user details. 
○ Automate or manually create accounts, ensuring correct roles are assigned. ● Offboarding and Maintenance: 
○ Monitor lists of inactive or departing users; promptly remove or deactivate accounts to maintain system security per technology guidelines. 
2. Course and Content Management 
2.1 Create and Manage Course Templates 
● Develop standardized course homepages and shells for consistent appearance and navigation across courses. 
● Update templates annually or as needed to incorporate new requirements or features. 2.2 Organize and Structure Course Content 
● Utilize modules to group related content, activities, and assessments. ● Provide guidelines or checklists to instructors to ensure consistent module usage and naming conventions. 
2.3 Collaborate with Instructors and Instructional Designers 
● Offer consultative services for designing in and out of class experiences.
● Provide best practices for creating or importing content (e.g., quizzes, assignments, discussion boards). 
3. Technical Support and Troubleshooting 
3.1 Provide Tier 1 and Tier 2 Support 
● Serve as a primary contact for Canvas-related questions from faculty, staff, students and parents. 
● Escalate advanced technical issues to vendor support or internal teams as needed. 3.2 Diagnose and Resolve Issues 
● Monitor Canvas status (e.g., from vendor dashboards or announcements). ● Document common issues and solutions to build an internal knowledge base. 
4. Data and Analytics 
4.1 Generate and Analyze Reports 
● Use Canvas analytics to monitor learner progress, engagement, and course effectiveness. 
● Share data insights (e.g., enrollment trends, grade distributions) with leaders. 4.2 Leverage Admin Analytics 
● Examine institution-wide usage metrics (e.g., login frequency, time spent). ● Identify opportunities for targeted intervention based on student performance. 
5. Integration and Customization 
5.1 Ensure Seamless Integration with External Tools 
● Configure and maintain integrations for third-party publishers, etc. 
● Coordinate with the technology and SIS Administrator for compatibility and security checks. 
5.2 Customize the User Interface and Branding 
● Align Canvas look and feel with the institution’s identity standards. 
● Test customizations thoroughly to avoid negatively impacting user experience. 5.3 Set Up and Manage Sub-Accounts 
● Organize sub-accounts (colleges, departments, or grade levels) to ensure consistent structure.
● Delegate sub-account administrative rights to department leads if necessary. 6. Training and Support 
6.1 Develop and Deliver Personalized Training 
● Provide role-specific training for administrators, teachers, students and parents. ● Offer periodic refreshers or onboarding sessions each semester or academic year. 
6.2 Create Documentation and Resources 
● Publish training guides, video tutorials, and FAQs in a central knowledge repository. ● Update documentation as Canvas rolls out new features. 
6.3 Stay Updated on New Canvas Features 
● Regularly review Canvas release notes and training webinars. 
● Communicate relevant changes or enhancements to the wider institution.
