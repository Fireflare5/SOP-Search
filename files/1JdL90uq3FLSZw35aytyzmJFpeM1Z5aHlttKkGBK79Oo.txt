


This SOP outlines the processes, roles, and responsibilities involved in developing, implementing, and managing AI solutions within the organization. Key areas include: 
● Project Planning and Initiation 
● Data Management and Governance 
● Model Development and Deployment 
● Security, Privacy, and Compliance 
● Monitoring and Maintenance 
● Training and Support

1.1 Identify Educational Use Cases 
● Collaborate with teachers, instructional coaches and the Teaching and Learning team to determine potential AI applications that align with district learning objectives (e.g., adaptive learning, automated feedback tools, resource scheduling). 
● Evaluate feasibility in terms of hardware availability, grade-level appropriateness, and relevant privacy regulations (e.g., FERPA, COPPA). 
1.2 Proposal & Approval 
● Draft a brief project proposal outlining scope (e.g., pilot program, full-scale adoption), potential benefits to students, and anticipated costs. 
● Present the proposal to district leadership for formal approval, ensuring it meets educational goals and policy standards. 
2.1 Student Data Collection & Privacy 
● Collect only the data necessary for the AI project (e.g., attendance, grades, or limited demographic information). 
● Comply with FERPA, COPPA, and any state-level student privacy laws; anonymize or de-identify data wherever possible. 
2.2 Data Storage & Access 
● Use secure, district-approved storage solutions for any student data sets used in AI model training or real-time operation. 
● Restrict access to student-level data to authorized personnel (e.g., District tehcnology, AI Director) with oversight from school leadership. 
3.1 Age-Appropriate Design
● Ensure AI tools and models are tested for relevancy and safety at different grade levels. ● Involve educators and, where possible, small groups of pilot students in the user-experience testing phase to gather feedback on content appropriateness. 
3.2 Pilot Testing & Scalability 
● Start with a controlled pilot in a small set of classrooms or grade levels to collect feedback and measure effectiveness (e.g., improvement in reading skills, math proficiency). 
● If results are positive, scale up to more classrooms or the entire district, with a phased approach to training teachers and support staff. 
3.3 Deployment & Integration 
● Integrate AI tools with existing K-12 systems (e.g., Learning Management Systems, Student Information Systems) to minimize user friction. 
● Coordinate with the district technology department to set up secure sign-on processes (SSO) and appropriate user permissions for teachers, students, and administrators. 
4.1 School Network Safeguards 
● Collaborate with the district technology department to apply strict network security measures (firewalls, content filtering) when deploying AI solutions. 
● Periodically audit third-party AI tools for compliance with district network standards and child-appropriate content filters. 
4.2 Compliance with Child Safety Standards 
● Maintain compliance with local, state, and federal child protection laws (e.g., COPPA, CIPA). 
● Implement transparency measures for parents/guardians (e.g., consent forms, clear usage policies) when AI solutions handle student data. 
4.3 Incident Response 
● Develop a clear plan to address security breaches or misuse of AI systems. ● Notify relevant parties (parents, principals, district leadership) promptly if a breach impacts student data or privacy.
5.1 Performance Tracking & Feedback 
● Regularly measure student engagement and academic progress tied to AI-assisted activities (e.g., reading fluency, math benchmarks). 
● Collect teacher and student feedback to identify usability or equity issues (e.g., students with limited technology access). 
5.2 Periodic Model Updates 
● Schedule updates or retraining for AI models to address changes in curriculum standards or to incorporate new student data. 
● Ensure these updates are approved and communicated in advance to avoid disruptions in classrooms. 
5.3 Ongoing Evaluation & Adjustments 
● Use a cycle of continuous improvement: gather data on student outcomes, revise AI implementations accordingly, and maintain open feedback channels with teachers and principals. 
● Document lessons learned for future AI projects or expansions. 
6.1 Staff & Educator Training 
● Provide targeted professional learning for teachers and administrators on AI functions, data usage, and best practices. 
● Offer ongoing training resources (e.g., webinars, in-person workshops, training manuals) to keep staff up-to-date on evolving AI features. 
6.2 Student & Parent Orientation 
● Communicate benefits and basic usage guidelines of AI tools with students and parents/guardians during open houses, newsletters, or orientation sessions. ● Provide age-appropriate guidance for students on digital citizenship and responsible AI use. 
6.3 Help Desk & Ongoing Assistance 
● The central point of contact to respond to teacher or student issues quickly is the Director of AI and BL.
● Maintain an up-to-date FAQ or troubleshooting guide for common AI-related questions and problems.
