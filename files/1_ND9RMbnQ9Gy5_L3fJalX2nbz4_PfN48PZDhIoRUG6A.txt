


To ensure a consistent, research-based, and standards-aligned curriculum for elementary education in the Lindbergh School District, this Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the annual curriculum writing and revision process. 
● Curriculum Authors: 
a. Conduct annual reviews and updates. 
b. Revise curriculum in response to Missouri Learning Standards changes. c. Ensure alignment with district instructional expectations. 
d. Collaborate with instructional coaches and curriculum coordinators. 
● Instructional Coaches & Curriculum Coordinators: 
a. Provide support and guidance during curriculum development. 
b. Ensure instructional alignment across grade levels. 
c. Assist in professional development related to curriculum implementation. 
Annual Curriculum Review and Writing Process: 
● Summer Curriculum Review (Annually): 
○ Curriculum authors will analyze priority standards and proficiency scales for each content area. 
○ Authors will update the curriculum based on changes to Missouri Learning Standards and district instructional priorities. 
○ The curriculum will be reviewed for alignment with research-based best practices and state assessments. 
● Full Curriculum Revision (As Needed): 
○ If Missouri Learning Standards undergo significant changes, curriculum authors will conduct a full review and revision of the affected content area(s).
○ Revisions will be implemented in collaboration with instructional coaches and curriculum coordinators. 
Curriculum Document Components: Each curriculum document will include the following essential components: 
● Priority Standards: Essential learning expectations derived from Missouri Learning Standards. 
● Supporting Standards: Additional standards that reinforce and support mastery of priority standards. 
● Proficiency Scales: Clearly defined levels of student mastery for each priority standard. ● Scope and Sequence: A structured timeline for instruction, ensuring coherent and logical progression. 
● Lifelong Transfer Goals: Statements outlining how students will apply their learning beyond the classroom. 
● Common Assessments: Formative and summative assessments to measure student proficiency and inform instruction. 
● T&L Administrative Assistant: Creates and manages curriculum templates, controls editing access, ensures formatting compliance, and updates the district website with approved documents. 
● Curriculum Authors: Develop and revise curriculum following the provided templates and formatting guidelines. 
● Executive Director of Elementary Education: Review completed curriculum documents and approve access requests for revisions. 
● Board of Education (BOE): Grants final approval for newly developed or revised curriculum. 
1.1 Initiation 
1. When a grade level or subject area is due for new or updated curriculum, the T&L Administrative Assistant: 
○ Creates standardized templates for each relevant course. 
○ Stores these templates in the Teaching & Learning (T&L) Google Shared Drive. 2. Writers are assigned and given temporary editing access to these documents during writing sessions.
1.2 Writing Guidelines 
1. Writers must adhere to the district's formatting guidelines for curriculum documents. 2. Hyperlinks should not be added to the curriculum documents. 
3. Writers must complete their assigned sections within the designated timeframe. 1.3 Review & Approval 
1. Once a course guide is completed, it is submitted for review by the Executive Director of Elementary Education and curriculum coordinators. 
2. Upon review completion, the T&L Administrative Assistant removes editing access from the writing group. 
3. Documents undergo a final review before submission to the Board of Education for approval. 
4. After BOE approval, the T&L Administrative Assistant: 
○ Uploads the new curriculum documents to the district website. 
○ Removes outdated curriculum documents. 
Timeline and Approval Process: 
● March-April: Identify curriculum authors for each grade level. 
● May-June: Provide training and resources for curriculum authors. 
● June-July: Conduct curriculum review and writing sessions. 
● August: Finalize and approve curriculum documents for implementation. ● Ongoing: Monitor implementation, gather feedback, and make necessary mid-year adjustments. 
1.4 Documentation and Accessibility: 
● All curriculum documents will be stored in a centralized digital platform for accessibility by educators. 
● Updates and revisions will be communicated to all stakeholders, including teachers and administrators. 
1. If revisions are needed in the future, curriculum writers must submit a request for editing access through the Executive Director of Elementary Education. 
2. Upon approval, the T&L Administrative Assistant grants temporary editing access for necessary updates.
3. If changes are made and require BOE approval, the process follows Section 1.3 Review & Approval. 
4. Once approved, the T&L Administrative Assistant updates the website with the revised curriculum.
