


This SOP establishes a standardized process for sorting and storing textbooks and curriculum kits at the end of the school year across the six elementary schools in the district. It ensures proper organization, transfer, and storage in preparation for the next school year, considering: 
● Retiring teachers. 
● Teachers changing grade levels or classrooms. 
● Section removals due to decreased enrollment. 
● Section additions due to increased enrollment. 
● Support from central office, warehouse, and facilities staff for moving materials. 
Elementary School Teachers 
● Sort textbooks and curriculum kits based on retention, redistribution, or disposal guidelines. 
● Label and pack materials according to the established storage and transfer system. 
Principals 
● Identify classrooms closing or opening based on enrollment changes. ● Approve and track inventory adjustments and Work Orders. 
Administrative Assistant or Building Assistant (Principal appointed champion) 
● Oversee the sorting and organization of materials within the building (each grade level, subject area, etc). 
● Coordinate with the principal and T&L Department on the reassignment of curriculum materials. 
● Submit Work Orders for transfers out of building as needed (example: Benchmark materials at Long, boxed and labeled from Melissa Mabry’s classroom - work
order created for pick up at from Long and delivery to Sappington for new 4th grade section, teacher TBD) 
● Submit Work Orders for transfers within building as needed. 
Support Service Center and Facilities Staff 
● Transport boxes between schools or to central storage upon Work Order request. ● Develop clear label/destination guidelines required for pick up. 
● Ensure accurate placement of materials based on school needs. T&L Department 
● Provide guidance on curriculum retention, redistribution, and disposal. ● Ensure compliance with district and state educational resource policies. 
● Teachers categorize materials into three groups: Retain, Transfer, or Dispose. ○ Retain: Items that will remain in the same classroom for next year. ○ Transfer: Materials needed in another classroom, school, or grade level. ○ Dispose: Outdated or damaged materials following district disposal policies. 
● Retained Materials: 
○ Store neatly in labeled cabinets or classroom storage areas. 
● Transferred Materials: 
○ Pack securely in building-provided boxes. 
○ Label each box with: 
■ Teacher’s Name 
■ Current Grade Level 
■ New Destination (Teacher/School/Grade Level) 
■ Contents Description 
● Disposal Materials: 
○ Follow district recycling/disposal guidelines. 
○ Submit a Work Order if items require bulk removal. (again Craig/Tara, do we want teachers submitting Work Orders?) 
● Teachers complete a Work Order form for materials requiring transportation. ● Work Orders must include: 
○ Pickup location 
○ Delivery destination 
○ Box count and contents 
○ Contact information 
● Submit Work Orders to the building principal for approval before sending them to the warehouse team? 
● Principals cross-check inventory changes with curriculum needs. ● Support Service Staff confirm deliveries and report any discrepancies to principals and T&L. 
● T&L maintains a district-wide log of resource distribution. 
● SOP distributed by Executive Director of Elementary Education and Executive Director or Secondary Education to principals and staff via email by May 1. 
● School-wide meeting to review procedures in mid-May. 
● Work Orders must be submitted by the last week of school. 
● Final storage and transfers completed by the first week of summer break. ● Changes in hiring over the summer addresses on as needed basis.
