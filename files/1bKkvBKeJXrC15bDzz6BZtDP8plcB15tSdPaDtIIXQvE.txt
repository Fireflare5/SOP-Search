


This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the process for curricular acceleration at Lindbergh Schools, ensuring appropriate educational pathways for students whose academic needs extend beyond standard classroom differentiation. This SOP covers subject-level and grade-level acceleration, including referral, evaluation, implementation, and review processes. 
This SOP covers: 
● Referral and initial review for acceleration. 
● Evaluation criteria and assessment procedures. 
● Development and implementation of acceleration plans. 
● Ongoing monitoring and review of acceleration effectiveness. 
● Procedures for addressing situations where acceleration is not a successful fit.

Initial Referral and Review (Level I: Differentiation): 
● Teacher or parent/guardian identifies a student who may require acceleration. ● Teacher consults with the PLC/MTSS team to review student data and implement classroom differentiation strategies. 
● PLC/MTSS team evaluates the effectiveness of differentiation strategies. ● If differentiation is successful, the team develops a plan for ongoing differentiation. ● If differentiation is not successful, or if the team feels that there is enough data, proceed to level 2. 
Acceleration Evaluation (Level II: Subject Acceleration, Level III: Grade Acceleration): 
● If differentiation is insufficient, the teacher or parent/guardian notifies the school administrative team, who notifies the Director of Gifted Programs.
● Subject Level Acceleration: 
○ Student data is reviewed, including standardized test scores (95th percentile or higher), classroom assessments, and executive functioning/maturity 
assessments. 
○ The Director of Gifted Programs works with the PLC/MTSS team to determine if subject acceleration is appropriate. 
○ For math acceleration, if the student will complete Algebra 2 before 9th grade, the Math Acceleration form must be submitted to DESE by the Director of Gifted Programs: https://dese.mo.gov/personalized-accelerated-math-plan 
● Grade Level Acceleration: 
○ Student data is reviewed, including IQ tests (95th percentile or higher), standardized test scores (95th percentile or higher), and emotional 
maturity/executive functioning assessments. 
○ The Iowa Acceleration Scale is used to determine eligibility and shared with the PLC/MTSS team. 
○ An acceleration committee is created to determine eligibility, if needed. ● Parent permission is required. 
● The Acceleration Determination and Approval Form is completed (Educlimber) Acceleration Plan Development and Implementation: 
● If acceleration is approved, an acceleration plan is developed by the teacher, school administrator, and Director of Gifted Programs (or acceleration committee) and housed in Educlimber. 
● The plan includes strategies for supporting the student's transition and success. ● The plan is shared with the parent/guardian for approval. 
● The Acceleration Plan form is completed in Educlimberand implemented. Ongoing Monitoring and Review: 
● The PLC/MTSS team (or acceleration committee, as needed) reconvenes regularly to monitor the student's progress. 
● Data is collected to assess the effectiveness of the acceleration plan. ● Adjustments are made to the plan as needed. 
● For subject acceleration, the team consults after a grading period, reviewing ○ Analysis of student academic progress. 
○ Evaluation of student social-emotional well-being. 
○ Input from the student, parents, and teachers. 
● For grade acceleration, monitoring continues through the first year in the following areas: ○ Analysis of student academic progress. 
○ Evaluation of student social-emotional well-being. 
○ Input from the student, parents, and teachers.
● The Director of Gifted Program updates acceleration forms in educlimber at the end of each school year and at the beginning of each school year to record growth and progress, teacher assignments, and any anecdotal records. 
Review Process for Acceleration That Is Not a Successful Fit: 
● If, during the monitoring and review process, the team determines that acceleration is not meeting the student's needs, the team, including the parents, will consider: ○ Returning the student to their original grade level or subject area. 
○ Implementing alternative differentiation strategies. 
● A revised plan is developed and implemented, outlining the transition and support strategies. 
Documentation and Record Keeping: 
● All documentation related to the acceleration process is maintained in the student's permanent record. 
● This includes referral forms, evaluation data, acceleration plans, and review documentation. 
This SOP will be distributed to all district and building administrators, counselors, and the gifted department. 
Information about acceleration will be available on the district website. 
Parents/guardians will be notified of all decisions related to their child's acceleration. 
Regular communication will occur between parents, teachers, and the Director of Gifted Programs.
