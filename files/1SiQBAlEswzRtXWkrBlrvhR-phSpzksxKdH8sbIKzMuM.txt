


Ensure consistent and age-appropriate use of movies for instruction while following copyright law 
● Chief Academic Officer - Assures yearly purchase of copyright access through SWANK licensing 
● Principal - Approves building-level processes 
● Teacher - Previews any movies, follows procedures and communicates with the principal and families, as necessary 
Teachers and staff may access and show any approved media via SWANK public performance license; this includes anything purchased, rented, streamed, or borrowed personally or via the district, so long as it is a legal avenue. 
Movies must be previewed by the teacher and directly related to curriculum in order to be shown. In general, showing movies should be limited in scope; clips should be substituted, as appropriate. 
Ratings 
● G: General audiences, all ages admitted 
● PG: Parental guidance suggested, all ages admitted 
● PG-13: Parents strongly cautioned; some material may be inappropriate for children under 13. 
● Unrated: Those made prior to the advent of the MPAA rating system, foreign films, and videos produced specifically for educational purposes


Grade Span Access 
● ECE: G-rated only 
● K-5: Universal access to G-rated; PG ratings require a signed permission slip ● 6 - 8: Universal access to G-rated; PG ratings require parent notification; PG-13 require a signed permission slip 
● 9:12: Universal access to G-rated and PG ratings; PG-13 ratings require parent notification 
● PK-12: For unrated movies, teachers must use their best professional judgment in showing these films as it relates to the curriculum and the age level of the students. 
Principals will determine any exceptions to be made to these procedures. 
Integrate expectations into building handbooks. 
SWANK - T&L purchases an annual license for all schools. This Public Performance Site License allows your school to legally exhibit motion pictures.
