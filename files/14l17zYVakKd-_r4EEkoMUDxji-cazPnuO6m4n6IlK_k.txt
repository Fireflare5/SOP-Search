


This SOP outlines the processes, roles, and responsibilities involved in selecting, purchasing, implementing, managing, and troubleshooting online textbooks within the district. Key areas include: 
● Textbook Evaluation and Adoption 
● Acquisition and Licensing 
● Textbook Deployment and Technical Integration 
● Vendor Management and Issue Resolution 
● Training and Support 
● Monitoring and Continuous Improvement 
Someone: 
● Oversees online textbook evaluation and adoption processes. 
● Coordinates licensing agreements, budgeting, and vendor relationships. ● Ensures textbooks align with instructional objectives and technology requirements. 
Technology and Director of AI and BL: 
● Manages deployment, integration, and technical troubleshooting of online textbooks. ● Addresses technical and system compatibility issues promptly. 
Instructional Coaches: 
● Provide input on curriculum alignment and textbook effectiveness. 
● Support educators in integrating textbooks into instructional practices. Teachers and Support Staff: 
● Implement online textbooks effectively within classrooms.
● Participate in training and provide timely feedback on functionality and accessibility. 
Textbook Evaluation and Adoption 
● Collaborate with educators to identify curricular needs and textbook requirements. ● Coordinate with technology and the Director of AI and BL to evaluate textbook options for content accuracy, ease of use, compatibility, and accessibility. 
● Conduct pilot tests to identify potential issues before large-scale adoption. 2. Acquisition and Licensing 
● Review vendor terms thoroughly to ensure they meet district standards and comply with legal requirements (FERPA, COPPA). 
● Clearly document licensing terms and communicate expectations with vendors. ● Secure necessary district approvals and purchase licensing aligned with district budget processes. 
3. Textbook Deployment and Technical Integration 
● Collaborate closely with the Technology Team for seamless textbook integration into existing district platforms (LMS, SIS). 
● Schedule deployments strategically to minimize disruptions to instruction. ● Configure access (e.g., single sign-on, rostering) and user permissions accurately. 
4. Vendor Management and Issue Resolution 
● Establish clear vendor communication channels and escalation paths for resolving technical and content issues. 
● Maintain a log of issues reported, vendor responses, and resolution timelines. ● Hold periodic review meetings with vendors to address recurring issues and ensure compliance with service agreements. 
5. Training and Support 
● Schedule comprehensive training sessions for educators upon textbook adoption. ● Provide accessible resources including user guides, FAQs, and troubleshooting tips. ● Maintain a responsive support system to quickly address teacher and student issues. 
6. Monitoring and Continuous Improvement 
● Collect regular feedback from educators and students regarding textbook effectiveness and usability.
● Analyze usage data and feedback to determine renewal, upgrade, or discontinuation decisions. 
● Document and distribute best practices and lessons learned to improve future textbook adoption cycles.
