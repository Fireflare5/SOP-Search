


This document outlines the process for planning and attending professional learning that requires out of town travel. 
● Employees Attending Professional Learning: 
a. Ensure alignment with department or district goals. 
b. Retain all required receipts for reimbursement. Submit a signed travel and expense voucher to the district business office. 
c. Adhere to district conduct guidelines. 
● Supervisors and Administrators: 
a. Approve and verify budget allocation for professional learning. 
b. Review expense submissions for completeness and compliance. 
● Administrative Assistants: 
a. Purchase, collect receipts, and code applicable district made purchases for registration, hotel, airfare, or rental vehicles. 
Step 1: Investigate 
● Confirm alignment with department, building, or district compass plan. ● If leadership initiates attendance, proceed to Step 3. 
● ***Presenting at a conference does not guarantee payment by district. Step 2: Research 
● Estimate Costs: Include registration, meals, transportation, lodging, and substitute coverage. 
○ Carpooling is a fiscally responsible option and is strongly encouraged.
○ If a district employee decides to utilize their personal vehicle to travel for Lindbergh Schools business the reimbursement amount will be limited to the cost of renting a standard size vehicle. Use the vehicle cost calculator to compare cost; print/screenshot calculator to include with request to attend. 
● Budget Confirmation: Verify funding availability from appropriate budgets (Building PL, T&L Budget, etc.). 
● *** For EdPlus workshops or conferences contact Teaching & Learning Administrative Assistant to discuss registration and the cost. 
Step 3: Implement 
● Submission of Requests: 
1. Use Frontline Professional Growth for conference/workshop submissions. 2. Submit Absence Management requests if applicable. 
● Expense Management: 
1. Use district purchasing cards for major expenses. 
2. Secure pre-approved POs for expenses > $500. 
● Hotel Booking Steps: 
1. Reserve rooms with district cards. 
2. Submit a credit card authorization form. 
3. Notify attendees of incidental charges and receipt requirements. 
Step 4: Ongoing Activities 
● During travel: 
○ Attendee: maintain itemized receipts for all expenses. 
○ Share professional learning highlights on social media (optional and 
professional). 
Step 5: Completion 
● Post-travel: 
1. Complete Travel & Expense Voucher. 
2. Attach receipts and mileage calculator (if applicable). 
3. Submit all documentation within 30 days. 
● Supervisors ensure expenses comply with district policies. 
This SOP will be communicated to all relevant personnel during onboarding and before travel periods.
